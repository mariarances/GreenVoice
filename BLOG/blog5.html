Causes of Biodiversity Loss

📅 March 14, 2026 👤 John Ralph Villarosa ⏱ 2 Min Read

Understanding the major factors behind the decline of Earth’s biodiversity

The decrease in the variety of plants, animals, and microbes found in ecosystems is referred to as biodiversity loss. Human activity is the primary cause of this issue, which has detrimental effects on ecosystems and human life.

Encyclopedia Britannica

1. Habitat Loss and Destruction The biggest cause of biodiversity loss is habitat loss. This happens when natural environments like forests, wetlands, and grasslands are cleared for agriculture, urban development, and infrastructure. When habitats are destroyed or fragmented, species lose their homes, food sources, and breeding grounds, leading to population decline or extinction.

2. Overexploitation Overexploitation occurs when humans harvest species faster than they can reproduce. Examples include overfishing, hunting, and illegal wildlife trade. This leads to population decline and can drive species to extinction.

3. Pollution Pollution includes harmful substances introduced into the environment, such as chemicals, plastics, and waste. These pollutants can poison organisms, degrade habitats, and disrupt reproductive systems, contributing to biodiversity loss.

4. Climate Change Climate change, caused mainly by greenhouse gas emissions, alters temperature and weather patterns. These changes affect ecosystems, making it difficult for many species to adapt, survive, or reproduce.

5. Human Population Growth and Consumption The rapid growth of the human population and increased consumption of natural resources intensify all other causes. More land is converted for agriculture and urban use, increasing pressure on ecosystems.
